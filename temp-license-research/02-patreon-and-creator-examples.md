# Patreon and Creator-Level License Research

A look at how established 3D-printing creators structure their commercial
license tiers, mostly on Patreon and Patreon-adjacent platforms (Tribes,
Thangs, Printables Clubs). These licenses are the de-facto standard that
buyers expect in 2026, so my MakerWorld license should be in the same
ballpark.

## 1. Cinderwing3D (Patreon — large, well-known dragon studio)

Source: <https://www.patreon.com/cw/cinderwing3d>, gift page, and a public
Facebook post clarifying terms.

Key clauses:

- Commercial license is granted **only while the subscription is active**.
  Cancel = license ends. No grace period stated.
- Subscribers may sell **physical prints only**. Sharing or selling the
  STL/3MF/digital files is never allowed and triggers a permanent ban.
- Subscribers **must print the products themselves**. No outsourcing.
- **No mass production** and **no drop-shipping through third-party
  manufacturers**.
- After backlash with cheap mass-produced copies, Cinderwing3D **banned
  selling on Temu, Alibaba, and all drop-shipping platforms** by name.
- Modifications to the geometry require **per-request approval**. Approved
  modifications are still subject to credit and the no-file-sharing rule.
- Subscribers may **not use the creator's photos** for listings. They must
  use photos of their own prints. (Etsy is reportedly cracking down on
  duplicate stock photos, so this is also good for the seller.)
- Subscribers must complete an **Authorized Sellers Form** in the welcome
  post and may receive a "badge" for their listings.
- Credit/attribution to Cinderwing3D is expected on listings.

## 2. Patrick Miniatures (Patreon / MyMiniFactory Tribes)

Source: <https://patrickminiatures.de/pages/print-sell-patrick-miniatures-commercial-license>

Patrick Miniatures publishes the full license terms publicly, which is
useful as a structural template. Notable clauses:

- License is **non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable**.
- Tied to an active subscription.
- Termination is **strict**: on cancellation, expiration, or violation,
  the license is **immediately revoked**, and the subscriber must:
  - Stop all sales immediately.
  - Remove all listings from all platforms.
  - Cease distribution of all inventory, including previously printed
    items.
  - Not sell, gift, or transfer remaining stock.
  - **No grace period and no sell-off period.**
- All files and designs remain the **exclusive property** of the creator.
  License grants **physical-print sales only**, not ownership.
- **Non-exclusive**: the creator may grant the same license to many
  sellers in any region.
- **Pricing independence**: subscribers set their own prices. The
  creator's pricing is reference only.
- Account sharing is forbidden.
- Acceptance of terms is automatic on subscription.

## 3. Morethan3D (subscription tier with a softer grace period)

Source: <https://morethan3d.com/commercial-license-terms/>

Allowed while subscription is active:

- Print physical copies.
- Sell physical printed models to customers.
- Use printed models in photos, videos, and marketing.
- Use prints for commissions and custom orders.

Prohibited:

- Sharing, reselling, or redistributing STL or digital files.
- Uploading files to other marketplaces or file-sharing platforms.
- Modifying and reselling digital files.
- Claiming the designs as your own work.
- Using the creator's brand names, logos, or trademarks.
- Mass production via factories or injection molding.
- Selling digital prints, molds, or casts.
- Presenting the work as "official", "authorized", or "endorsed".

Notable: on subscription end, **existing physical inventory may be sold
for up to 30 days** after the license ends, but **no new prints**. This
is a friendlier middle-ground than Patrick Miniatures' "stop everything
now" stance.

## 4. Sawant3D (Patreon, smaller scale)

Source: <https://sawant3d.com/?page_id=970>

Concise, clear terms:

- License is a **monthly subscription**.
- **Royalty-free**: the creator does not charge a per-print fee.
- The **number of active commercial licenses is limited** (an interesting
  scarcity-based business model — probably overkill for me right now).
- Cancellation **revokes the right to sell**, even if the subscriber still
  has the source files.
- **No claiming the designs as your own.**
- Sell **prints only**, never the digital files.
- Attribution like "Designed by SAWANT3D" is welcome.
- The creator reserves the right to **cancel the license for users who
  break the rules**.
- **Unlimited print volume** while the subscription is active.

## 5. DM Stash (a more "merchant" style legal contract)

Source: <https://www.dmstash.com/merchant-terms/>

Stricter, more contractual language. Notable additions:

- License limits sales to **individual consumers only** (no wholesale,
  no B2B).
- Subscribers must legally own all models being sold and must have sourced
  them **only from the creator's authorized storefronts**. Unauthorized
  sourcing terminates the license.
- **Failure to maintain active subscription = immediate termination.**
- The agreement can be modified by the creator with a **21-day grace
  period** for compliance.
- The creator can **revoke an individual license immediately for
  material breach**, with no grace period.
- If the creator discontinues the program entirely, **existing active
  licenses remain valid through their cycle** but are not renewable.

## 6. General 2026 industry observations

Drawing from the broader 2026 guides (pea3d.com, printcal.co, modelrover.com):

- The "Merchant Tier" subscription model has converged across Patreon,
  MyMiniFactory Tribes, Thangs, Printables Clubs, and now MakerWorld.
- Typical pricing range: **$15–$50/month** for personal sellers, with
  higher tiers ($99–$300+) for print farms and larger businesses.
- Common subscriber pain points the license should address:
  - "Can I sell on Etsy / Amazon / eBay / Shopify / TikTok Shop?"
  - "Can I sell at in-person events / craft fairs / local shops?"
  - "Can I sell to a wholesaler?"
  - "Can I sell remaining stock after I cancel?"
  - "Can I outsource printing to a print farm?"
  - "Can I modify the model?"
  - "Do I have to credit the designer in the listing?"
- The clearest licenses spell each of these out explicitly. Vagueness is
  the #1 source of disputes.
- Creators converge on banning **Temu, Alibaba, drop-shipping, and
  third-party manufacturers** because these channels devalue everyone's
  work and the creator has no quality control.
- It's standard to require that the **subscriber, not the creator, is
  liable for product safety** of the physical prints they sell.

## 7. What I want to take from these

I want the final license to be:

- Short, plain-language, and readable on a phone (this is a personal
  site, not a B2B SaaS).
- Structurally aligned with the MakerWorld helper template — same eight
  headings as the helper, in roughly the same order.
- Augmented with the most important Patreon-style protections:
  - Non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable.
  - License = one subscription = one seller / account.
  - Print-yourself default, with a small print-farm carve-out via written
    permission.
  - No mass production / no drop-shipping / no Temu / no Alibaba.
  - Modest sell-off period for existing physical inventory (e.g. 30 days)
    once a subscription ends — this is friendlier and matches the
    Morethan3D model.
  - No brand impersonation (no "official", "authorized", "endorsed").
  - Liability is the seller's.
  - Cross-reference the MakerWorld Membership Agreement for billing /
    refunds / dispute behavior so I don't have to redefine those.
